BEST FURNITURE
The first known furniture is said to have existed in the Egyptian civilisation about 4000 years ago, though it might have existed in some crude form prior to that. The Egyptians arc said to have used chairs, tables, stools, chests, beds etc.

Furniture in Greenbelt A Sign of the Tough Times A view of the front of a typical housing project in the planned community of Greenbelt.
The concept of furniture first developed as early as 3100-2500 B.C. The first items created for household use were made of stone, as wood was not readily available during the Neolithic time period. Dressers, cupboards and beds were amongst the first forms of furniture. The dresser was said to be the most important piece of furniture in the beginning, as they faced the entrance of each house in Neolithic society and often displayed carved artwork of symbolic objects.
